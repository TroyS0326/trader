Transparency FAQ

What does the Transparency page measure?
It reports paper-mode performance metrics only when the public sample-size threshold is met, and explains building-state transparency before public metrics are active.
Why are metrics hidden at first?
To avoid misleading tiny-sample statistics. Public metrics activate only after enough completed trades exist.
Are the metrics based on live capital?
Unless clearly labeled otherwise, metrics represent paper-mode/simulated workflow data, not live-capital performance.
How does paper mode differ from live routing?
Paper mode helps test logic without live capital. Live routing uses real market conditions and can be affected by fills, spreads, liquidity, delays, and broker/account configuration.
How does XeanVI use AI-assisted validation?
AI-assisted validation can help organize catalyst/context signals against the user’s playbook. It is a workflow signal, not financial advice or a guarantee.
Does XeanVI hold my funds?
No. Funds remain with the supported broker. XeanVI is not a custodian and does not withdraw or transfer user funds.
Can I audit why a workflow moved forward?
XeanVI is designed to provide dashboard and audit visibility around playbook rules, validation context, risk settings, and workflow status where available.
Is this financial advice?
No. XeanVI provides software for rule-driven trading workflows. It does not provide financial advice, investment recommendations, legal advice, tax advice, guaranteed fills, or guaranteed results. Trading involves risk, including possible loss of capital.
